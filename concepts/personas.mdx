A **persona** is a simulated buyer or user profile that Profound uses to send prompts to AI assistants. Rather than querying ChatGPT or Perplexity from a generic, anonymous perspective, personas give each prompt a realistic human context—an age, a location, a job title, a way of researching decisions. Because AI assistants tailor their answers based on who they think is asking, personas are what make your visibility data truly representative of your real-world audience.

## Why Personas Matter

Different buyers get different answers. A 28-year-old startup founder researching CRM software in Austin will receive a different AI response than a 52-year-old IT director at a Fortune 500 company in London asking the same question. If you only track one perspective, you're missing the full picture of how your brand appears to your actual audience.

<Tip>
  Start with three to five personas that reflect your highest-value buyer
  segments. You can always add more as your tracking matures—but a small,
  well-defined set produces cleaner insights than a large, overlapping one.
</Tip>

Profound uses personas across two levels of your data model:

- **Organization-level personas** apply as the default across all categories and prompts in your organization.
- **Category-level personas** override the organization default for a specific category, letting you target the exact audience relevant to that topic.

## Persona Fields

### Demographics

<ResponseField name="personaId" type="string" required>
  Unique identifier for the persona.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="name" type="string" required>
  A descriptive label for the persona (e.g., `SMB Founder - US`, `Enterprise IT Director - UK`).
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="age" type="number">
  The persona's age. Affects how AI assistants frame recommendations, tone, and assumed technical familiarity.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="gender" type="string">
  The persona's gender identity. Used where AI assistants may adjust language or product framing.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="location" type="string">
  City, region, or country (e.g., `Austin, TX`, `London, UK`). Influences local recommendations, regulatory context, and regional pricing references in AI answers.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="language" type="string">
  Preferred language for queries, expressed as a BCP 47 locale code (e.g., `en-US`, `fr-FR`).
</ResponseField>

### Employment

<ResponseField name="jobTitle" type="string">
  The persona's professional role (e.g., `VP of Sales`, `IT Director`, `Small Business Owner`). One of the strongest signals influencing how AI models contextualize answers.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="industry" type="string">
  The industry the persona works in (e.g., `Financial Services`, `Healthcare`, `Retail`). Shapes AI answers that reference sector-specific regulations, integrations, or use cases.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="companySize" type="string">
  Size of the persona's employer, typically expressed as an employee range (e.g., `1–10`, `500–5,000`, `10,000+`). Affects AI recommendations around pricing tiers, scalability, and feature depth.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="seniorityLevel" type="string">
  Seniority or decision-making authority (e.g., `Individual Contributor`, `Manager`, `C-Suite`). Influences whether AI assistants emphasize strategic value or tactical implementation details.
</ResponseField>

### Behavior

<ResponseField name="researchStyle" type="string">
  How the persona typically approaches research decisions (e.g., `Comparison-Driven`, `Peer-Recommendation-Led`, `Analyst-Report-Focused`). Helps Profound craft prompts that reflect realistic search intent.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="techSavviness" type="string">
  The persona's comfort with technology: `Low`, `Medium`, or `High`. Affects how AI assistants pitch complexity and use jargon in responses.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="buyingStage" type="string">
  Where the persona is in the purchase journey: `Awareness`, `Consideration`, or `Decision`. Aligns prompts with the types of questions buyers ask at each stage.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="painPoints" type="string[]">
  A list of core challenges this persona is trying to solve (e.g., `["reducing churn", "onboarding complexity", "data silos"]`). Used to inform prompt phrasing and topic targeting.
</ResponseField>

## Organization-Level vs. Category-Level Personas

<Note>
  Organization-level personas serve as the default for every category and prompt
  in your organization. You only need to assign category-level personas when a
  specific segment has a meaningfully different audience.
</Note>

### Organization-Level Personas

Organization-level personas represent the broadest view of your audience. They apply to all categories that don't have their own persona assignments. Use organization-level personas for buyer profiles that are relevant across your entire product portfolio.

### Category-Level Personas

Category-level personas let you narrow the audience for a specific tracking segment. For example, if your organization tracks both a developer-tools category and an executive-buyer category, you'd assign a `Software Engineer` persona to the former and a `CTO` persona to the latter—ensuring each category's prompts reflect the right buyer perspective.

When a category has one or more personas assigned, those personas fully replace the organization-level defaults for that category.

## Persona Scope at a Glance

| Scope | Applies To | Overrides Org Default? |
|---|---|---|
| Organization-level | All categories with no persona assignment | — |
| Category-level | Prompts within a specific category | ✅ Yes |

## Fetching Personas

Use the Profound TypeScript SDK and REST API to retrieve personas at the organization or category level.

<CodeGroup>

```typescript List Organization Personas
import Profound from '@profoundai/client';

const client = new Profound({ apiKey: process.env.PROFOUND_API_KEY });

// Retrieve organization details and then fetch personas via the API
const organizations = await client.organizations.list();
const orgId = organizations[0].organizationId;

const response = await fetch(
  `https://api.tryprofound.com/v1/organizations/${orgId}/personas`,
  { headers: { Authorization: `Bearer ${process.env.PROFOUND_API_KEY}` } }
);
const personas = await response.json();

for (const persona of personas) {
  console.log(`${persona.name} — ${persona.jobTitle}, ${persona.location}`);
}
```

```typescript List Category Personas
import Profound from '@profoundai/client';

const client = new Profound({ apiKey: process.env.PROFOUND_API_KEY });

// Retrieve categories and then fetch personas scoped to a category
const categories = await client.organizations.categories.list();
const categoryId = categories[0].categoryId;

const response = await fetch(
  `https://api.tryprofound.com/v1/organizations/categories/${categoryId}/personas`,
  { headers: { Authorization: `Bearer ${process.env.PROFOUND_API_KEY}` } }
);
const categoryPersonas = await response.json();

for (const persona of categoryPersonas) {
  console.log(`${persona.name} — Buying Stage: ${persona.buyingStage}`);
}
```

</CodeGroup>

## Building Effective Personas

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify your key buyer segments">
    Start with your sales and marketing team's existing ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) definitions. Map each segment to a persona with distinct demographics, seniority, and buying behavior.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define employment context carefully">
    Job title, industry, and company size have the largest influence on AI answer variation. Be specific—`VP of Sales at a 200-person SaaS company` produces more differentiated data than `Sales Leader`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set realistic behavioral attributes">
    Choose a `researchStyle` and `buyingStage` that reflect how this buyer actually searches. A `Decision`-stage buyer asks different questions than an `Awareness`-stage one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign personas to the right categories">
    For categories with a specialized audience, assign category-level personas. For broadly applicable categories, rely on your organization-level defaults.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and iterate">
    After your first prompt run, compare AI answers across personas. Large divergences signal which audience segments experience the most different brand coverage—and where to focus your content strategy.
  </Step>
</Steps>
